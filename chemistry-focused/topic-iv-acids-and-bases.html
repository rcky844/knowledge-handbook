Concepts

Refer to: Acids and Bases

Definitions

Introduction to acids:

  • Acid: An acid ionizes in water to give hydrogen ion as the only cation.
  • Basicity is the maximum amount of ionizable hydrogen atom in an acid molecule.

Introduction to bases and alkalis:

  • Alkali is a water soluble substance (/ base) reacting with an acid to give salt and water only. / Alkali is a substance when dissolved in water to give hydroxide ions as the only anion. (HKDSE 2020 Paper 1B Q7(b))
  • Base: A base reacts with an acid to give salt and water only.
  • Neutralization is a chemical reaction where an acid reacts with a base to produce salt and water as the only products.

Strength of acid and alkali:

  • Strength of acid / alkali: The strength of an acid / alkali depends on the degree of ionization / dissociation of that acid / alkali.
  • Strong acid: A strong acid completely ionizes in water to give hydrogen ion as the only cations.
  • Weak acid: A weak acid partially ionizes in water to give hydrogen ion as the only cations.
  • Strong alkali: A strong alkali completely dissociates in water to give hydroxide ion as the only anion.
  • Weak alkali: A weak alkali partially dissociates in water to give hydroxide ion as the only anion. ("ionize" for ammonia)

Concentration and molarity of solution:

  • Concentrated solution: A concentrated solution is a solution with a high amount of solute dissolved in the solvent.
  • Saturated solution: A saturated solution is a solution that has dissolved all the solute it can at a given temperature.

HKCEE 2010 Paper 1 Q10(d)

With reference to the below graph, explain the temperature change of the mixture throughout the experiment when sulphuric acid is added into sodium hydroxide solution.

Answer:

  • As neutralisation is exothermic, temperature of the solution rose when sulphuric acid was added into sodium hydroxide solution.
  • When the sodium hydroxide was just completely reacted, the temperature reached a maximum value.
  • After that, the addition of excess (cold) sulphuric acid lowered the temperature of the reaction mixture.
