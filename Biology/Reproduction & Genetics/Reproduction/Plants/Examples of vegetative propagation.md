#### Stem tubers
*See also: [[Mechanisms of vegetative propagation]]*
- <u>Swollen</u> storage organ formed at the tip of an underground stem.
- **'Eye' on the surface**: Contains a bud and scar of a scale leaf.
- <u>The old tuber shrinks and dries up after the stem tuber develops aerial roots.</u>
**Examples**: potato, Chinese yam

#### Bulbs
![[bulbs.webp|250]]
- <u>Short, vertical underground</u> stem with layers of fleshy scale leaves.
- Scale leaves contain food reserves. It also encloses and protects the buds.
**Examples**: onion, tulip, daffodil

#### Corms
![[corms.webp|250]]
- <u>Short, vertical underground</u> stem swollen with food reserves.
- Usually covered by brown, protective scale leaves with no fleshy scale leaves.
**Examples**: *Gladiolus*, *Crocus*, water chestnuts

#### Rhizomes
![[rhizomes.webp]]
- <u>Horizontal</u> underground stem swollen with food reserves.
**Examples**: Ginger, *Iris*, lotus
